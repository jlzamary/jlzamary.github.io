Climate change is reshaping Alaska’s fragile ecosystems, with warming weather driving seasonal shifts that threaten coastal habitats. The Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) leads the Chukchi Sea Coastal Lagoon Monitoring project to provide insights that support researchers, conservationists, and local communities.

As a research analyst with William & Mary’s Institute for Integrative Conservation (IIC), I collaborated with WCS to study ice coverage, lagoon connectivity, and algal growth across 26 lagoons in the Chukchi and Beaufort Seas. Our goal was to develop an interactive ArcGIS Experience Builder application that enables fishers and conservation experts to track habitat changes and identify long-term trends through accessible spatial data.

Drawing on my background in biology and environmental science, I developed a Python workflow using the Google Earth Engine (GEE) API to process satellite imagery. I applied the Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) to delineate lagoon bodies and calculated the Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index (NDCI) from Sentinel-2 bands to assess algal blooms. NDCI values were scaled from –0.2 to 0.8 and visualized with a blue-to-red gradient to indicate bloom severity. Time-series datasets were exported as CSVs for WCS analysis, while selected satellite images were processed as GeoTIFFs for further work in ArcPro.

NDCI Python

def calculate_ndci(image):
    # Add Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index (NDCI) as a new band
    ndci = image.normalizedDifference(['B5','B4']).rename('NDCI')
    return image.addBands(ndci)
NDWI Python

def mask_land(image):
    # Generate water mask using the NDWI index and mask out non-water pixels
    ndwi = image.normalizedDifference(['B3','B8']).rename('NDWI')
    water_mask = ndwi.gt(0.1)
    return image.updateMask(water_mask)

As of September 2025, the project is ongoing. The satellite-derived datasets and interactive mapping tools provide critical resources for monitoring ecosystem health and informing conservation strategies in Arctic lagoons.
